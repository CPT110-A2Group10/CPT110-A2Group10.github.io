Area 4 - Arduinon, Raspberry Pi and Makey Makey

This is a brief description of Arduino, Raspberry Pi and Makey Makey. The Arduino and Raspberry Pi are both micro controllers with its own microprocessor chip. They are like a mini-computer that can run programs to control electronic components. Arduino and Raspberry Pi have developed different models, each model having specific assemblage components. The Arduino and Raspberry Pi motherboards have I/O pins to attach other electronic devices. They cannot do anything without being programmed. The program codes are input into the Integrated Development Environment (IDE), once the code is complete it can be downloaded to the microprocessor via USB ports.

Arduino is Opensource microcontroller, both software and hardware are Opensource too. This means that it is available to anybody to copy. There are many companies that have created their own cheaper versions of Arduino. This is great for anyone that wants to make an electronic project. There has been many amazing projects created using the Arduino, which can be seen in the Arduino project hub webpage (https://create.arduino.cc/projecthub). To create a project the Arduino boards can be bought by itself or in kits. The Arduino shields are circuit boards that allow for additional capabilities which are can be bought separately. With additional shields other electronic parts can be added such as; sensors, motors and electronic displays. The Arduino IDE can be downloaded from the Arduino website (https://www.arduino.cc/). Arduino use its own version of C programming language and the extensive coding libraries that is free to use. The downside with Arduino its small memory capacity and it can only run one program at a time. Also, if you want to have Bluetooth and other features Arduino Shields have to be added.

Raspberry Pi can run more than one program at a time. This is because it has its own Operating System (OS) which is free (https://www.raspberrypi.org/downloads/) and have its own desktop. From the desktop the user can choose different IDE to program. You have the option of choosing scratch for those who don’t have any experience in coding. Scratch was created by MIT to encourage and teach students to code in Python. For those with more experience Debian or IDLE Python are also included in the desktop. The most recent model Raspberry Pi 4 board has bigger memory up to 4G, three different type USB ports, ethernet, two micro HDMI, Bluetooth and wireless.

Makey Makey (https://makeymakey.com/) is a product created by MIT Media Lab. It is a two sided circuit board touchpad with six input sockets to attach wires. Once the board has been connected to a PC or laptop via USB, the alligator clips can be linked to any object that conducts electricity. By attaching these wires with alligator clips to everyday objects it turns them into touchpads. Makey Makey can be attached to Arduino and Raspberry Pi boards.

The Arduino, Raspberry Pi and Makey Mare are simple, but not too simple. They were built around the idea that students will be using these. Teaches future designers, artists, engineers and improved learning by building on ideas and sharing ideas. These micro-controllers make it possible to build affordable computers, which put digital making into the hands of people all over the world. These devices have proven that computing could be smaller and cheaper. There are many ways in which they have made positive contribution. It’s used in classrooms, used by hobbyist and within the industrial environment. People of all ages have used them to learn about computing and electronics. What makes them so appealing are many experiments that led to creating products we never could have imagined. 

Project - A Learning Management System (LMS)

The project is to create a desktop or mobile application library similar to the Open eBooks application (http://openebooks.net). This project is based on my belief that all children should have access to good education and good quality information. As we have all been told that one of the main pathways to economic success is the undertaking of additional years of education. The better educated are usually characterised by lower rates of unemployment, higher earnings, and more prestigious occupational standing.

UNESCO (http://www.unesco.org/new/en/gefi/priorities/quality-of-learning) points out that there are 57 million children out of school and 617 million youth worldwide lack basic mathematics and literacy skills. To begin with, I propose to create a project that targets local schools then hopefully go global.

Ben Edwards and Jennifer Baxter article (2014) examined access to educational services in the disadvantaged region in Australia, which shows that Australian children in regional areas have poorer learning outcomes for children living in these areas. Free access to an online library for secondary and primary schools and students. This library is like the way university online library works. The difference is that content will be age appropriate and will have a look of a search engine. Students will have 10 days free access to digital books, audios, pictures, and videos.

Currently, it is very difficult for students to do any proper research on the internet because they are constantly having to filter advertisements and fake or incorrect information. The current filter that is used in a government school doesn’t work. When students search for information online sometimes good websites are blocked and bad ones are not. In my many years of teaching experience, I still struggle to monitor and guide students while they research online. Often, I have directed students to websites that I have vetted, but because student access is restricted students are then left with information that I can’t be sure is correct or safe. The current government school online is not able to distinguish between good and bad online content. There are so many local libraries that offer online access to books, it would be great to be able to have access to them in a desktop or mobile application.

To set up this project several things that should be considered:

  • Technical staff and time are available for development.
  • Staff with a deep knowledge of the library system.
  • Affordability and price of the service.
  • Sharing the service with other organisation.
  • Who are the potential subscribers and how to manage their accounts?
  • What ebook content to be included?
  • Can undesired content be suppressed?
  • Service content subscriptions access to the full text.
  • How can we access digital collections and institutional repositories?
  • How much of the content is mutually licensed?

It is important to look at systems that are already in use like the one used by lots of university institution. Dallis and Popp (2012) explain the Web-scale discovery service (WSD) is a central search and retrieval interface index, which contain a library’s locally hosted and remotely licensed content.

The eLGAR's Smart System Project (Fletcher, B. 2009) in New Zealand showed the type of challenges and some consideration of what to expect when implementing this project. Although the NZ project is different from what is being proposed, there are similar requirements that are needed. Fletcher (2009) advises making use of professional outside help. For example, the legislative requirement of government bodies to provide this service and possible solutions were for funding. eLGAR project points out that the importance of high degree of cooperation with the government and a local organisation that will use and funds the project.

In developing this project I believe we should procure a software license from Open eBook or develop our own app that is similar. Also learn from organisations like Open eBook and Digital Public of America (https://dp.la/) so that we are able to have a better understanding of library systems. We have to make sure to "plan for the future and buy capacity to grow the system" (Fletcher, B. 2009).
