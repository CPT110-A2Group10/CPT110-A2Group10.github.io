Area 1 - Machine Learning

Machine learning is a subset of artificial intelligence, where algorithms improve themselves as they are fed data, finding patterns and figuring things out without explicitly being told.

Machine learning is still in its infancy but has already come a long way. It has already been used in the health industry to do tasks such as diagnosing eye disease and predicting hospital wait times, as well as many other applications (Vincent, 2018). Countries around the world are utilising it to increase the development of autonomous and semi-autonomous weapons for military use (Cronk, 2019). It is also being used in economics, with companies like Paypal announcing that machine learning is the future of credit scoring and fraud prevention (Shah, 2019). Machine learning has aided the creation of semi-automated cars, which have made their way onto roads recently, with fully automated cars to follow in the near future. The one application that has already impacted most people is machine learning in digital advertising. Targeted advertising is everywhere and algorithms are constantly finding new strategies that increase sales. As algorithms continue to improve, its predictive capabilities will increase in accuracy.

In the coming years machine learning will have a far-reaching global impact and as it is integrated into consumer products will become a part of daily life. Spending on artificial intelligence and machine learning is forecast to reach $35.8 billion globally in 2019, and to double by 2022 (IDC, 2018). If there is data available, machine learning is either already being utilised or looking to be.

Education and training will also feel a major impact as there will be a change in what jobs exist and what skills will be in demand. White-collar workers are the most likely to be displaced, and many jobs such as ones based around repetitive tasks that can be automated have already started being taken by machine learning. Jobs that require analysing and processing data will also be automated. Machine learning will replace jobs in almost every industry, but will also create new jobs. People-oriented jobs and are not likely to be replaced by machine learning.

These changes will affect my daily life, especially in regards to employment since I will be working in the IT industry. Area 2 - Autonomous Vehicles

Autonomous vehicles are vehicles that are capable of driving themselves with little to no human input. They use artificial intelligence combined with sensors to perform driver tasks.

There are multiple levels of automation and we have not yet reached them all. SAE (SAE International 2018) lists the five levels as Driver Assistance, Partial Automation, Conditional Automation, High Automation, and Full Automation. Semi-autonomous vehicles which are level two and function like a high-level cruise control requiring driver supervision are already on the roads, with fully autonomous cars expected to join them in the future. Major vehicle companies are investing billions of dollars into artificial intelligence software and tech for their autonomous vehicles (Walker, 2019).

Many automakers have announced timelines for their vehicles and what levels they expect to get to within the next few years. Ford is expecting to have a fully autonomous vehicle with no steering wheel by 2021, whereas Honda, Toyota and Hyundai are a bit less optimistic, aiming for cars capable of full autonomy when highway driving by 2020 (Walker). Tesla has been at the forefront of autonomous vehicles and already have cars with the required hardware, ready for when the software catches up to become fully autonomous (Walker).

The biggest impact autonomous cars will have will be the prevention of car accidents. There are around 1.25 million deaths globally due to motor vehicle accidents, 94% of which are caused by human error (Singh, 2015). Autonomous cars could prevent those accidents, saving hundreds of thousands of lives. Other major changes on our roads will be the automation of lift services, and possibly these services replacing the need to own your own car.

The research into autonomous vehicles is creating thousands of new jobs for engineers and computer scientists, and as research into the hardware and software required for high functioning artificial intelligence gets more funding it leads to improvements in other related fields, such as robotics and consumer technology.

In my daily life, autonomous vehicles will likely eventually replace my need for a car. It will also make the roads safer for myself and everyone else I know. The increased funding into artificial intelligence and machine learning will make its way into other fields and create new consumer technologies that don’t yet exist.

Area 3 - Natural Language Processing and Chatterbots

Natural language processing is the interaction between human and computer language that analyses and processes data. Although progress has improved over the years, there are still challenges with speech or accent recognition.

The first recorded work of natural language processing dates back to the early 1950s however there were talks of this technology prior to this. Alan Turing proposed the idea in an article titled Computing Machinery and Intelligence. This has become known as the Turing test originally published in 1950. In 1954 the Georgetown experiment involved the automatic translation of sixty Russian sentences into English. Throughout the 1960s further advancements in automatic translation moved slowly and continued right up until the late 1980s when machine learning algorithms were introduced. This breakthrough was due to several factors. First the increase in computing power with modern machines, and secondly with the lessening of the Chomskyan theory of linguistics. The theoretic underpinnings of the Chomskyan theory discouraged the corpus linguistics that is required for the machine learning of language processing.

In more recent years, representation learning and deep neural network style machine learning methods have become common in natural language processing. This is due to the state of the art results and techniques. An example of this is the use of word embeddings, which captures properties of words and even question answering. The most common algorithms that are being used now are focussing on unsupervised or semi-supervised programs. These particular algorithms are able to learn from data that has not been predetermined. Risks with this particular technology rise with machines often using unreliable information from the internet.

A chatbot, or more commonly chatterbox, is a computer program that was designed to perform conversationally as a human would. Uses for the chatbot include phone answering software, marketing, education, games, and online virtual assistants, etc. The first chatbot was created by Michael Mauldin in 1994 when he designed ‘JULIA’, a chatbot development software kit mostly used by Microsoft. This was based heavily on ELIZA, a 1966 program designed by Joseph Weizenbaum. ELIZA was considered believable as a human however was certainly not intelligent and could only speak pre-programmed phrases. Technology for the chatbot is based on the works of Alan Turing, however at this stage in 2019 still does not have the capabilities required to pass the Turing test.

Area 4 - Arduinon, Raspberry Pi and Makey Makey

This is a brief description of Arduino, Raspberry Pi and Makey Makey. The Arduino and Raspberry Pi are both micro controllers with its own microprocessor chip. They are like a mini-computer that can run programs to control electronic components. Arduino and Raspberry Pi have developed different models, each model having specific assemblage components. The Arduino and Raspberry Pi motherboards have I/O pins to attach other electronic devices. They cannot do anything without being programmed. The program codes are input into the Integrated Development Environment (IDE), once the code is complete it can be downloaded to the microprocessor via USB ports.

Arduino is Opensource microcontroller, both software and hardware are Opensource too. This means that it is available to anybody to copy. There are many companies that have created their own cheaper versions of Arduino. This is great for anyone that wants to make an electronic project. There has been many amazing projects created using the Arduino, which can be seen in the Arduino project hub webpage (https://create.arduino.cc/projecthub). To create a project the Arduino boards can be bought by itself or in kits. The Arduino shields are circuit boards that allow for additional capabilities which are can be bought separately. With additional shields other electronic parts can be added such as; sensors, motors and electronic displays. The Arduino IDE can be downloaded from the Arduino website (https://www.arduino.cc/). Arduino use its own version of C programming language and the extensive coding libraries that is free to use. The downside with Arduino its small memory capacity and it can only run one program at a time. Also, if you want to have Bluetooth and other features Arduino Shields have to be added.

Raspberry Pi can run more than one program at a time. This is because it has its own Operating System (OS) which is free (https://www.raspberrypi.org/downloads/) and have its own desktop. From the desktop the user can choose different IDE to program. You have the option of choosing scratch for those who don’t have any experience in coding. Scratch was created by MIT to encourage and teach students to code in Python. For those with more experience Debian or IDLE Python are also included in the desktop. The most recent model Raspberry Pi 4 board has bigger memory up to 4G, three different type USB ports, ethernet, two micro HDMI, Bluetooth and wireless.

Makey Makey (https://makeymakey.com/) is a product created by MIT Media Lab. It is a two sided circuit board touchpad with six input sockets to attach wires. Once the board has been connected to a PC or laptop via USB, the alligator clips can be linked to any object that conducts electricity. By attaching these wires with alligator clips to everyday objects it turns them into touchpads. Makey Makey can be attached to Arduino and Raspberry Pi boards.

The Arduino, Raspberry Pi and Makey Mare are simple, but not too simple. They were built around the idea that students will be using these. Teaches future designers, artists, engineers and improved learning by building on ideas and sharing ideas. These micro-controllers make it possible to build affordable computers, which put digital making into the hands of people all over the world. These devices have proven that computing could be smaller and cheaper. There are many ways in which they have made positive contribution. It’s used in classrooms, used by hobbyist and within the industrial environment. People of all ages have used them to learn about computing and electronics. What makes them so appealing are many experiments that led to creating products we never could have imagined.
